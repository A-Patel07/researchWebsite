Using a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) Network to Forecast the Future Trophic State of Lakes

Background

Lakes serve many purposes, such as residential water sources, irrigation, recreation, and as habitats for various marine life. However, eutrophication promotes harmful algal blooms (HABs). HABs can compromise the water quality of lakes and be detrimental to marine ecosystems. One consequence of eutrophication is a decrease in dissolved oxygen levels caused by the decomposition of algae. The decrease creates conditions in which some marine organisms cannot survive, resulting in a decline of biodiversity. In addition, eutrophication has been associated with an increase in harmful cyanobacteria.

Rationale

Current methods for forecasting eutrophication include simulations and machine learning (ML) models. One disadvantage of simulations is their need for numerous data of a water body and its environment for calibration and verification. This process can be costly and time-consuming. In addition, simulations require a deep understanding of its processes to effectively calibrate. For example, the Environmental Fluid Dynamics Code required field data such as temperature, nutrient decay rates, and nutrient flux rates. Previous ML models to forecast eutrophication, although successful in predicting algal biomass, have not considered both total nitrogen and total phosphorus as inputs. This study proposes an LSTM model to forecast the future chlorophyll-a, total phosphorus, and total nitrogen levels of lakes based on the past data. Using these forecasted values, the trophic state index value and trophic state of the lake is calculated.

Results

The accuracy of the training data was evaluated by calculating the mean square error (MSE) value at each epochs, and it was observed that the MSE value decreased from 0.137 in the first epoch to 0.058 in the 200th epoch. Similarly, the accuracy of the validation data was evaluated, which was the same as the test data. The MSE value at different epochs was calculated, and it was observed that the MSE value decreased from 0.113 in the first epoch to 0.056 in the 200th epoch. After the model was evaluated on the test data. The model was able to forecast future TSI values accurately as the mean absolute error was 3.904 and mean absolute percent error was 5.365%.
